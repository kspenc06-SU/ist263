Best Tourist Attractions in Nashville!

Ryman Auditorium is the “Mother Church” of music, bringing thousands of artists and fans together. Home to the famous Grand Ole Opry, Ryman has become the most famous stage in the world. Performers of different music genres have made their way to the Ryman, from Johnny Cash to Harry Styles.


Centennial Park is one of Nashville’s most popular parks, and includes historical monuments, an Art Center, and other activities such as a volleyball court, a dog park, and a walking trail.
It was home to the 1897 Tennessee Centennial Exposition, a festival to commemorate the
100th anniversary of Tennessee joining the Union. This event led to the creation of the
Parthenon replica, which is an exact copy of the one in Athens, Greece. This monument is home to the park’s art museum.


Bridgestone Arena is mainly used as the home stadium for the Nashville Predators of the NHL. This stadium has also hosted concerts and other entertainment performances. Opened in 1996, Bridgestone has already had over 13 million guests come through its doors. Performers such as Taylor Swift, Elton John, and the Rolling Stones have performed at this venue.
